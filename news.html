Rbot

Very naturally communicates, jokes, flirts, asks for a hug, invites girls to dinner, reads the inscriptions from the badges. A memorable, fun robot. But this is a telepresence robot, and a modest operator is sitting nearby. It is he who translates all the thoughts that the robot voices.

Cheetah

Another high-profile development of Boston Dynamics of those years — a fleet-footed robot Cheetah ("cheetah"), which on the treadmill developed a speed of 29 miles per hour (46.6 km/h) — faster than Usain Bolt. Boston Dynamics was not limited to quadrupeds. Under a contract with DARPA, the company was developing a two-legged robot called Atlas to compete in a robot competition run by the Pentagon. The purpose of the competition is to create a class of robots that can work in the conditions of natural and man-made disasters.

Ameca

Ameca is the world’s most advanced human shaped robot representing the forefront of human-robotics technology. Designed specifically as a platform for development into future robotics technologies, Ameca is the perfect humanoid robot platform for human-robot interaction.

Yandex robot

Yandex.rover is a half-meter courier robot for transporting small loads. The robot can carry up to 20 kg. The company began developing it in the summer of 2019 and then tested the robot at its headquarters. The robot is completely autonomous: it plans its own route, assesses the situation around, avoids obstacles and passes pedestrians and animals. He is able to drive on city sidewalks at a speed of about 5 km/h, can work both in summer and winter, fulfilling orders in different weather conditions.

Robot-Einstein

Robotics has advanced so much that scientists have managed to resurrect Einstein himself! Perhaps this will happen in the future, but for now we will have to settle for a robocopy of a scientist. Meet Albert Hubo– an android with a humanoid body. There are CCD cameras mounted in Albert's head for visual recognition of people, and a naturalistically executed face expresses a wide range of emotions. The Hubo body is a standard design for robots, the invention can move and speak.

SpotMini – удивительная робособака

At first glance, the invention of the developers of Boston Dynamics can be called similar to a four–legged faithful friend with a big stretch - the robot has no fur, no tail, or even a full-fledged head. However, do not rush to conclusions. SpotMini is trained in the habits of a dog, he is smart, perfectly navigates the terrain and overcomes obstacles, and is also able to work in a team with his fellows – for example, to open and hold the door for them. This is what made a splash in the field of robotics.

Geminoid DK – клон психолога

This Japanese robot was made to order in Japan in the likeness of a psychology professor from Aalborg University in Denmark. The teacher entertains students by conducting classes together with his mechanical double – this allows him to study the features of interaction between a human and a robot. There are rumors that a robotic copy of Henrik Scharf is often mistaken for the professor himself – at rest, Geminoid DK is really indistinguishable from the "original". However, the magic is destroyed when the robot begins to move or demonstrate emotions – the effect of the "sinister valley" is triggered.

Paro Baby Seal – робот-тюлень

Who would have thought that a soulless mechanism would cause emotion and a desire to embrace. It is for such feelings that a therapeutic toy designed for emotional uplift and improvement of well-being in the elderly and patients with autism is designed. If you can resist the charm of this baby seal without flinching, you are most likely a sociopath.

Chuchu – the world's first robo butterfly

This unique Japanese creation combines the fragility of natural beauty and the result of an accurate mathematical calculation. A robo-insect fluttering in a glass jar cannot be distinguished from the real one with the naked eye. The mechanical butterfly reacts to the tapping of fingers on the lid of the vessel.

Origami from biomaterial

The development of engineers from Sheffield, Massachusetts and Tokyo educational institutions will save lives. This crumb, consisting of a particle of pig intestines, selflessly dives into a person's stomach to get swallowed objects. The unusual structure in the form of origami allows the robot to fold and take a variety of shapes.
